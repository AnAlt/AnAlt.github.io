Order of Play

On the first round the dealer starts and no trading occurs. When a round ends, the loser (person with the most points) starts. Then it starts going clockwise and the next person will play a card. To play a card, first make sure if you are playing one card if it is singles, two cards for doubles, and so on, then make sure it can be played on whatever card was just played like when going up, 6s can play on 3s and when going down Js can play on Ks. Finally make sure that the power of the previous card and the card you are playing allows you to play that card.

Winning

To win, you must end with the least amount of points. The game ends when someone plays all of their cards and usually this means they win, but if someone else has less points, the person with less points will win. Also when you play all of your cards, you may not end by playing a joker.

Trading

The winner (person with least amount of points) and loser (person with most points) will trade cards. The winner will ask for a card, but not the suit and the loser must give that card if they have it. If the loser has multiple of that card, they can decide which to give. The winner continues to ask until they have taken a card and then they will return a card.

The dealer will deal the cards out and the rest of the deck will be put in the middle to take from. If a card is flipped up accidentally, then the person will keep it if they want to.

Evolving/Devolving

To evolve or devolve, you must play the cards next in the sequence. Also you need to play doubles on singles, triples on doubles and so on and you have to play an even number. For example, you can play double Qs on a single J when the order is going up and double 5s on a 6 when going down. To devolve, it is the opposite and you must play a odd number. Aces are both odd and even.

Taking Cards

When a card is played, if the same number is played on it, then the next person has to take 2 cards. If the next person has that card though, the can give 3 cards to the next person and so on. For doubles, you double the cards taken and for triples, it is triple and so on. For example, if someone plays double 2s and the next person plays double 2s, then the next person takes 4 cards.

Taking Card Cap

To make sure that someone does not take too many cards at once, there is a card cap. For singles, the most someone can take at once is 4, for doubles, it is 8, for triples it is 12 and so on. This will also apply to straits, but do not apply to the cards taken from 6s or the 9♥️.
